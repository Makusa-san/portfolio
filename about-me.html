About Me

Hey, I'm Max - a UI designer and front-end developer based in Vancouver. Originally from Russia, I moved to Canada a few years ago and have been studying Interactive Arts and Technology at SFU, where I've been learning to turn ideas into experiences that feel natural, structured, and alive.

My work sits at the intersection of design and development. I enjoy shaping how people understand and move through digital spaces, whether that's a bakery website or a 3D room planning tool. I believe design should speak for itself, quietly guiding users and creating something meaningful beyond just functionality.

Most recently, I worked as the UI/UX designer on RoomCraft, a web-based interior design tool built during SFU Surge's StormForge program. The project won the Surge Choice Award, which was a meaningful milestone in my growth as a designer.

Outside of design, I'm into photography, mostly phone shots of nature and the city, and I've been getting more into cooking and fitness since moving to Vancouver. I like finding structure and creativity in everyday things, which probably says something about how I approach design too.

I'm currently looking for a co-op position in UI/UX design or front-end development where I can contribute to a product team, keep learning, and help building interfaces that people actually enjoy using.
