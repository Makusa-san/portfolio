MaPastry — Bakery Website

Role UI Designer, Front-End Developer
Tools Figma, HTML, CSS
Type Website, University Project
Date Feb - Mar 2026

Intro

The goal of this project was to design a website for MaPastry, an imaginary Canadian bakery that uses maple syrup as its signature element to represent national food culture. The intention was to create a digital experience that feels warm, inviting, and culturally grounded, while remaining clear and easy to navigate for both local and international audiences.

Research & Development

The process began with developing the brand identity. I explored how MaPastry could communicate comfort, tradition and a welcoming atmosphere through visuals. This included selecting a warm, earth-toned color palette inspired by baking and natural materials, and combining expressive and readable typography to balance personality with clarity.

I also experimented with the landing and About Us pages, refining the elements that tell a story about MaPastry's values and the process of producing maple syrup, allowing users to better understand the concept and identity of the brand.

As the project developed, I focused on structuring content and designing layouts that guide users through the website in a natural and intuitive way, ensuring that key information such as products and MaPastry's brand story is easily accessible.

Final Design

The final outcome is a website coded with HTML and CSS that balances warmth and structure, translating a cultural concept into a clear and meaningful digital experience. I continued to refine hierarchy, spacing and interactive elements to support usability and visual consistency across all pages.

Challenges

One of the main challenges was translating an abstract cultural concept into a clear and engaging digital experience. While the idea of using maple syrup as a symbol of Canadian identity provided a strong foundation, it required careful design decisions to avoid relying on clichés while still communicating a recognizable theme.

Another challenge was balancing warmth and personality with clarity and usability, particularly when combining expressive visual elements with structured layouts. Coding the final design in HTML and CSS also presented challenges in maintaining visual consistency across pages while implementing responsive and functional layouts.

Reflection

Through this project, I learned how to balance visual expression with usability, especially when translating a cultural concept into a digital interface. Through exploration and iteration, I became more confident in making design decisions based on design principles rather than just aesthetics.

It reinforced my understanding that thoughtful design can guide attention, communicate values, and shape how users engage with a digital product.

If I were to approach a similar project again, I would spend more time exploring layout variations and testing how different structures affect user experience. This would allow me to make more confident design decisions earlier in the process and create a stronger connection between the brand identity and its digital presentation.
